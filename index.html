Steven Lonn, Ph.D.

Steven Lonn is the Assistant Director of the USE Lab and the Library Learning Analytics Specialist in the Digital Media Commons at the University of Michigan. His research focuses on the perceptions and use of information and learning technologies in education for collaborative teaching and learning and combining different data sources to arrive at concrete findings for evaluate, assessment, and future work.

Steven’s current research examines how learning management systems are used by instructors and students in higher education and how that use impacts pedagogy. This longitudinal research program not only details how current users perceive and interact with the technology, but also informs future design for these ubiquitous systems. Recently, Steven has led efforts to expand this research program across other institutions around the world using the Sakai community source collaborative learning environment.


Steven’s work has been published in Computers & Education and The Internet and Higher Education. He has presented at several conferences including the Annual Meeting of the American Educational Research Association, the International Conference of the Learning Sciences, and the International Conference on Computer-Supported Collaborative Learning.
